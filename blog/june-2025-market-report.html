June 2025 Treasure Valley Real Estate Market Report

📊

As we head into summer 2025, the Treasure Valley real estate market continues to show resilience — but the conditions are shifting in ways that matter for both buyers and sellers. Here's a clear-eyed look at what the data shows across Boise, Meridian, Eagle, Nampa, Caldwell, and the surrounding communities CWRE Idaho serves.

The Big Picture: June 2025

Inventory has ticked up compared to last year, giving buyers more options and slightly more negotiating room. At the same time, demand remains strong — particularly for well-priced, move-in-ready homes in the $350K–$500K range. Homes that show well and are priced accurately from the start are still selling quickly.

"In June, correctly priced homes in the Treasure Valley are still seeing multiple offers within the first week. The market hasn't gone soft — it's just become more selective." — Clint Walker

What This Means for Buyers

If you've been sitting on the sidelines waiting for prices to drop significantly — that doesn't appear to be coming in the near term. However, rising inventory means you now have more choices and more time to make decisions than you did in 2022 or 2023.

  • Get pre-approved now if you're planning to buy in the next 90 days
  • Look at slightly less popular zip codes (Kuna, Caldwell, Middleton) for better value
  • Don't skip the inspection contingency — the market isn't so hot that you need to waive it
  • Work with a local agent who knows off-market opportunities

What This Means for Sellers

Pricing discipline is more important than ever. Overpriced listings are sitting — sometimes for 60+ days — while accurately priced homes in good condition are still moving in 2–3 weeks. If you're thinking of selling in the next 6 months, now is a great time to start preparing.

  • Price based on recent comparable sales — not the peak prices of 2022
  • Invest in professional photography and minor staging
  • Address deferred maintenance before listing — buyers are more inspection-savvy
  • Discuss timing strategy with your agent — summer inventory competition is real

Looking Ahead: Summer & Fall 2025

Idaho continues to attract relocating buyers from higher-cost states, particularly California, Washington, and Oregon. This migration trend shows no signs of slowing and continues to support long-term demand across the Treasure Valley. Interest rates remain the biggest variable — any movement down could trigger a significant wave of pent-up demand.

My outlook for the remainder of 2025: a stable, slightly seller-favored market in most price ranges, with continued appreciation in the 2–4% range year-over-year. Exceptional properties in Eagle, Star, and North Meridian will continue to perform best.
